Qualitative value

Current routing algorithms are missing an entire category of information when evaluating possible routes: the aesthetic beauty and quality of the drive. This is where Road Scanner comes in. Our purpose is to leverage state of the art machine learning techniques to enable travelers to optimize their travel experience by providing a qualitative rating of a route’s beauty.

scenic route scoring

24 scenic routes were identified from online community sources, and the non-scenic routes were identified by our team’s local Californians. We used these data to train a highly accurate Deep Neural Network that scores the similarity of the user routes to the reference data.

We confirmed the validity of the model by rendering the attention layer maps for some high-confidence samples. We found that that the network learned to confidently detect the non-scenic pictures by the fact that the sky starts where the road ends, which is an indication of low interest features or generally featureless pictures.

Custom Trip Conditions

Most of the Google Street View images are shot in spring or summer, but in reality, people travel every day throughout the year and want to know what to expect at any given moment. We ended up training three CycleGANs to generate the missing pictures.

CycleGANS are based on pix2pix, an image to image translation network architecture, but with the difference of not requiring paired training data. Over time, our CycleGANs learned to map the latent space between the two training domais.

User Interface

Our final task was to put it all together into a website, where the users can specify any route and get back the route’s score, encoded similarly to any map traffic colors.

Moreover, the user can interact with the map and see samples of how the view would look like in all four different trip conditions, Normal, Winter, Dusk, Night.

Under the hood

To train our models, we downloaded over 20,000 Google Street View images from known scenic and non-scenic routes in California. California offers a large diversity of terrain, making it suitable for the vast majority of the possible views. Our scenic routes include views of mountains, lakes, oceans, deserts, rolling hills, and even wine country. The variability of the terrain will allow us to train a model that can generalize to other locations across the globe using pictures from just one state.
